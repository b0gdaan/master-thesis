\section{Appendix F: Market-Event Case Studies}
\label{app:market_events}

This appendix provides illustrated case studies of eleven landmark market events between
2020 and 2026, examined through the lens of the thesis's central variable: the rolling
Pearson correlation between Bitcoin and conventional assets.
Each episode is accompanied by a four-panel diagnostic figure, approximate price-change
statistics, and links to contemporary news sources.
The purpose is to anchor the aggregate quantitative results of Chapter~4 in concrete
historical episodes and to demonstrate how the regime-transition problem manifests
in practice.

\subsection*{Interpretation}

The eleven episodes collectively support four conclusions that complement the
aggregate quantitative findings of Chapter~4:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Macro risk-off events create the largest correlation spikes.}
    COVID (2020), Liberation Day (2025), and the 2021--22 Fed tightening cycle all
    pushed BTC--equity correlation above 0.60 for sustained periods. These are
    precisely the episodes where rolling-RMSE peaks in Figure~\ref{fig:rolling_rmse}.

    \item \textbf{Crypto-idiosyncratic shocks leave equity correlations largely unchanged.}
    The China mining ban, Luna collapse, and FTX bankruptcy each caused severe Bitcoin
    losses while equity markets barely moved, producing at most a brief, transitory
    correlation spike.

    \item \textbf{The institutional era (post-2024 ETF approval) sustains higher correlations.}
    The structural inflow of institutional capital via ETF wrappers means Bitcoin is
    now traded alongside growth equities in the same risk-on/off frameworks. The
    BTC--NASDAQ correlation has averaged higher since January 2024 than in any
    comparable prior 12-month window.

    \item \textbf{Rare safe-haven episodes exist but are short-lived.}
    The SVB crisis produced a genuine negative BTC--equity correlation, but it lasted
    only two to three weeks before reverting. This transience explains why persistence-based
    models continue to dominate out-of-sample: the autoregressive signal is strong
    enough to absorb regime shifts after a short lag, even if it cannot anticipate them.
\end{enumerate}
